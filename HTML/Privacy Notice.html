Data Protection

Privacy Notice

This Privacy Notice explains how Active Adventure Home Childcare collects, uses, protects, shares, and retains personal data in accordance with UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018, and our safeguarding responsibilities.

United Kingdom GDPR Notice Effective: 24 April 2026 Version 1.0

On this page

1. Overview 2. Who we are 3. Data we collect 4. Children and special category data 5. Legal basis 6. How we use data 7. Sharing information 8. Retention 9. Security 10. Your rights 11. Safeguarding override 12. Website and cookies 13. Contact and complaints

We protect family and child data with the same care we bring to childcare.

Because our services may involve children, health information, safeguarding notes, care routines, and household details, we treat privacy as a core part of professional care delivery.

1. Overview

This Privacy Notice applies when you visit our website, make an enquiry, request a booking, use our services, communicate with us, join a mailing list, apply for work, or otherwise provide personal information to Active Adventure Home Childcare.

It explains what information we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, who we may share it with, how long we keep it, and what rights you have under UK data protection law.

Important: This Privacy Notice is designed for a UK childcare provider handling sensitive family and child information. It should be reviewed by a qualified UK solicitor or data protection adviser before publication.

2. Who we are

Active Adventure Home Childcare is responsible for deciding how and why personal data is processed for the purposes described in this notice. For data protection purposes, we will usually act as the data controller.

Business

Active Adventure Home Childcare, a premium home childcare service operating in the United Kingdom.

Primary contact

Questions about privacy, care records, safeguarding records, or personal data should be sent to our contact email below.

Service context

We process information to manage childcare enquiries, bookings, care plans, safeguarding, payments, communication, and service delivery.

Children’s data

Where we process information about children, we do so with additional care because children’s data requires stronger protection.

3. Data we collect

The information we collect depends on how you interact with us and the services requested. It may include:

  • Identity data: names of parents, guardians, children, authorised collectors, emergency contacts, and relevant household members.
  • Contact data: email addresses, telephone numbers, home addresses, hotel addresses, booking locations, and emergency contact details.
  • Booking data: service type, booking date, care duration, location, staff preferences, payment status, cancellation history, and service notes.
  • Child profile data: age, routines, interests, school information, sleep routines, food preferences, behavioural needs, communication needs, and developmental support needs.
  • Health and safety data: allergies, medication requirements, medical conditions, disability information, dietary requirements, emergency plans, and relevant risk information.
  • Safeguarding data: incident notes, welfare concerns, contact restrictions, court orders, safeguarding disclosures, behavioural risks, and professional observations where necessary.
  • Payment and transaction data: invoices, billing information, payment confirmations, refunds, outstanding balances, and related correspondence.
  • Communication data: emails, forms, messages, call notes, feedback, complaints, service requests, and handover information.
  • Website data: IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, referral source, cookies, and analytics information where used.

4. Children and special category data

Our service may require us to process children’s personal data and special category data, including health, disability, behavioural, developmental, or safeguarding information.

We only collect this information where it is relevant, proportionate, and necessary for safe care delivery, safeguarding, legal compliance, or professional risk management.

Health information

Used to understand allergies, medication, emergency instructions, dietary restrictions, medical routines, and care needs.

Additional needs

Used to make reasonable adjustments and assign appropriate care professionals where possible.

Safeguarding notes

Used to record welfare concerns, incidents, professional observations, or information needed to protect a child.

Household information

Used where relevant to access, safety, authorised collection, emergency planning, or contact restrictions.

5. Legal basis for using personal data

Under UK GDPR, we must have a lawful basis for processing personal data. Depending on the context, we may rely on:

  • Contract: to respond to enquiries, confirm bookings, deliver childcare, manage payments, and provide agreed services.
  • Legal obligation: to meet safeguarding, tax, accounting, employment, insurance, or regulatory duties.
  • Legitimate interests: to operate, improve, protect, and manage our service, prevent misuse, resolve disputes, and maintain professional records.
  • Consent: where consent is required, including certain marketing communications or optional information.
  • Vital interests: where processing is necessary to protect someone’s life or safety in an emergency.
  • Substantial public interest / safeguarding: where processing is necessary to protect a child or vulnerable person.

Where we process special category data, we will rely on an additional condition under UK data protection law, such as explicit consent, vital interests, legal claims, employment obligations, safeguarding, health and social care, or substantial public interest where applicable.

6. How we use data

We use personal data to provide a safe, structured, premium childcare service and to meet our legal and safeguarding responsibilities.

  • Respond to enquiries and provide service information.
  • Create, confirm, amend, manage, and cancel bookings.
  • Prepare care plans, handover notes, risk assessments, and staffing requirements.
  • Assign suitable care professionals based on the child’s needs and service requirements.
  • Communicate with parents, guardians, emergency contacts, hotels, venues, and authorised third parties.
  • Process invoices, deposits, refunds, and payment administration.
  • Maintain safeguarding, safety, incident, complaint, and quality records.
  • Comply with tax, accounting, insurance, employment, legal, and regulatory obligations.
  • Send essential service communications, including booking confirmations, service updates, policy updates, and safety notices.
  • Send marketing updates where permitted by law or with your consent.

7. Sharing information

We only share personal data where necessary, lawful, and proportionate. Depending on the service, information may be shared with:

  • Assigned care professionals who need relevant information to provide safe and appropriate care.
  • Emergency contacts or authorised adults named by you.
  • Safeguarding bodies, local authorities, police, NHS or emergency services where necessary for child protection, welfare, safety, or legal reasons.
  • Professional advisers such as solicitors, accountants, insurers, auditors, or compliance advisers.
  • Service providers supporting booking systems, payment processing, website hosting, email, secure storage, analytics, or business operations.
  • Hotels, venues, travel partners, or schools where relevant to access, handover, collection, safety, or service coordination.

We do not sell personal data. We do not allow third parties to use your information for their own marketing unless you have given permission or the law allows it.

8. Data retention

We keep personal data only for as long as necessary for the purpose it was collected, including service delivery, safeguarding, legal, insurance, accounting, dispute resolution, and regulatory purposes.

Retention periods may vary depending on the type of information. Safeguarding, incident, complaint, and child welfare records may need to be kept for longer than routine enquiry or marketing data because they may be needed to protect children, evidence decision-making, or respond to legal claims.

Retention principle: We regularly review the information we hold and securely delete, anonymise, or archive information when it is no longer needed.

9. Data security

We use appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, misuse, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

  • Restricted access to care records and sensitive information.
  • Confidentiality expectations for staff and care professionals.
  • Secure digital systems where appropriate.
  • Limited sharing of child and family information on a need-to-know basis.
  • Practical safeguards for records, communications, and handover notes.

No system is completely risk-free. If we become aware of a personal data breach, we will assess the risk, take steps to contain it, and notify affected individuals or the Information Commissioner’s Office where required by law.

10. Your rights

Under UK data protection law, you may have the following rights depending on the circumstances:

  • The right to access personal data we hold about you.
  • The right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • The right to request deletion of personal data in certain circumstances.
  • The right to restrict how we use your data in certain circumstances.
  • The right to object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.
  • The right to data portability where applicable.
  • The right to withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

We may need to verify your identity before responding to a rights request. Some rights are not absolute, particularly where we must keep information for safeguarding, legal, insurance, accounting, or dispute-resolution reasons.

11. Safeguarding override

Safeguarding is a priority. Where we believe a child or vulnerable person may be at risk, we may record, use, and share relevant information without consent where this is necessary and lawful to protect welfare, comply with safeguarding duties, or support an investigation.

This may include sharing information with local authority safeguarding teams, police, NHS services, emergency services, schools, social care professionals, regulators, or other appropriate agencies.

Where possible and appropriate, we will be transparent with families. However, we may not provide advance notice where doing so could increase risk, prejudice an investigation, or conflict with legal or safeguarding obligations.

12. Website, cookies and marketing

Our website may collect limited technical data through cookies, analytics tools, forms, or similar technologies. This may help us understand website usage, improve performance, respond to enquiries, and maintain security.

If we use non-essential cookies, analytics, or marketing tools, we will provide appropriate information and, where required, obtain consent. You can usually control cookies through your browser settings.

We may send marketing communications about services, availability, insights, or updates where you have opted in or where the law permits. You can unsubscribe from marketing at any time. We may still send essential service communications relating to bookings, safety, policies, invoices, or care arrangements.

13. Contact and complaints

If you have questions about this Privacy Notice, want to exercise your data rights, or have concerns about how your information has been handled, please contact Active Adventure Home Childcare.

Email: jeffery.owusu@ojstrattech.co.uk

You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office, the UK supervisory authority for data protection matters. We would appreciate the opportunity to address your concern first where appropriate.

Updates to this notice

We may update this Privacy Notice from time to time to reflect changes in law, service structure, safeguarding practice, technology, or business operations. The latest version should be published on our website.
