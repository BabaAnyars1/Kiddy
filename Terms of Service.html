1. Overview

These Terms of Service apply to childcare, home support, travel support, hotel-based support, overnight care, educational support, specialist support, and related services provided or arranged by ELITE ANCHOR Home Childcare.

Our services are designed to provide premium, structured, professional childcare support. They are not a substitute for emergency medical care, statutory social care, specialist clinical treatment, or legal guardianship.

Where a separate written service agreement, booking confirmation, safeguarding policy, privacy notice, or care plan applies, those documents should be read alongside these Terms. If there is a conflict, the specific written booking confirmation or signed agreement will usually take priority for that booking.

Important: This page is drafted as a strong commercial template for a UK childcare business. It should be reviewed by a qualified UK solicitor before publication or client use.

“Client”, “you”, “your”

The parent, guardian, family member, company, hotel, travel partner, or authorised adult requesting or receiving services.

“Child” or “children”

Any child or young person for whom care, support, supervision, or related services are requested.

“Care Professional”

A confirmed request for services, including the agreed date, time, location, care requirements, and fee structure.

“Care Plan”

Any written or digital information used to guide service delivery, including routines, medical notes, behavioural needs, safeguarding considerations, and emergency contacts.

3. Eligibility and account information

You must be at least 18 years old and legally authorised to arrange childcare for the child named in the booking.

You agree to provide accurate, complete, and current information during enquiry, registration, booking, payment, onboarding, risk assessment, and care planning.

You must notify us promptly of any information that could affect safe service delivery, including allergies, medical conditions, additional needs, safeguarding concerns, court orders, contact restrictions, behavioural risks, medication requirements, or changes in collection authority.

4. Bookings and care plans

A booking is only confirmed when we issue written confirmation and any required deposit, prepayment, onboarding information, or signed agreement has been received.

We may decline, pause, amend, or cancel a booking where we reasonably believe the service cannot be delivered safely, lawfully, professionally, or in the best interests of the child.

Minimum booking periods

Minimum booking periods may apply depending on the service tier, location, time of day, staffing requirements, travel time, overnight arrangements, or specialist care needs. Any minimum period will be stated at booking or in the relevant service page.

Care information

You are responsible for ensuring that care information is truthful, complete, and updated before each session. We may require an onboarding call, written care plan, risk assessment, or additional professional review before confirming certain bookings.

5. Fees, payment and late payment

Fees are calculated according to the service selected, care professional assigned, booking duration, location, time of day, specialist requirements, travel requirements, and any additional services agreed.

  • All fees must be paid by the deadline stated in the booking confirmation or invoice.
  • Deposits, retainers, or prepayments may be required to secure availability.
  • Additional time, late finishes, travel, parking, congestion charges, entry fees, activity costs, meals, hotel access fees, and other reasonable expenses may be charged where applicable.
  • Late payment may result in suspension of future bookings, cancellation of reserved availability, recovery of debt collection costs, and statutory interest where legally permitted.

Unless expressly stated otherwise, quoted prices do not include third-party costs, venue costs, travel tickets, premium activity fees, or additional expenses incurred for the child.

6. Changes, cancellations and refunds

Cancellation and amendment rules are designed to protect professional staffing, reserved availability, travel arrangements, and operational planning.

Client cancellations

Cancellation charges may apply depending on how close the cancellation is to the booking start time and whether specialist staff, travel, overnight care, or extended care has been reserved.

Short-notice changes

Changes to time, location, duties, number of children, or care complexity are subject to approval and may result in additional charges.

No access or no handover

If we cannot start because access, handover, accurate information, or an authorised adult is unavailable, the booking may remain chargeable.

Agency cancellations

If we need to cancel due to safety, illness, emergency, or circumstances beyond our control, we will aim to provide a suitable replacement, credit, refund, or alternative solution where appropriate.

Refunds, credits, or rescheduling are assessed according to the booking type, notice period, work already completed, expenses incurred, and any applicable written agreement.

7. Client responsibilities

You agree to provide a safe, respectful, and appropriate environment for children and care professionals.

  • Provide accurate emergency contact details and ensure an authorised adult can be reached during the booking.
  • Ensure the care professional has safe access to the home, hotel, venue, or agreed location.
  • Provide necessary supplies, including food, nappies, medication instructions, clothing, keys, transport details, and comfort items where required.
  • Disclose pets, security systems, cameras, household risks, allergies, medical needs, behaviour plans, court orders, and any relevant safeguarding information.
  • Treat all care professionals with dignity, respect, and professional courtesy.
  • Do not request duties outside the agreed booking scope without prior approval.

We reserve the right to end a session or decline future services where a client, household member, guest, or third party behaves abusively, unsafely, unlawfully, or in a way that compromises professional boundaries.

8. Safeguarding, safety and health

Safeguarding is central to our service. We may take any action we reasonably consider necessary to protect a child, care professional, family member, or member of the public.

If we identify a safeguarding concern, immediate risk, non-disclosure, unsafe environment, medical emergency, or legal concern, we may contact emergency services, local authority safeguarding teams, health professionals, police, or other appropriate agencies.

Health and medication

You must provide clear written instructions for allergies, medication, medical routines, emergency plans, and health conditions. Care professionals may only administer medication or provide health-related support where it has been expressly agreed and is within their competence, role, insurance, and lawful authority.

Illness and infection control

You must disclose contagious illness, recent symptoms, or health risks before a booking. We may refuse or cancel a booking where illness creates an unacceptable risk to the child, care professional, household, or wider community.

9. Staffing and service delivery

We aim to assign care professionals whose experience matches the booking requirements. Specific individuals may be requested but cannot be guaranteed unless expressly confirmed in writing.

We may substitute a care professional where reasonably necessary due to illness, availability, travel disruption, safeguarding considerations, service suitability, or operational requirements.

Care professionals must not be privately engaged, solicited, hired, paid directly, or contacted for services outside ELITE ANCHOR without our prior written consent. Breach of this clause may result in cancellation of services and a reasonable introduction or placement fee.

10. Limitations of service

Our services are professional childcare and support services. Unless expressly agreed in writing, we do not provide:

  • Emergency medical treatment or clinical diagnosis.
  • Legal guardianship, parental responsibility, or statutory social care.
  • Unapproved transportation, high-risk activities, or unsupervised swimming.
  • Heavy domestic cleaning, personal assistant duties, or non-child-related household labour.
  • Services in unsafe, unlawful, intoxicated, abusive, or materially misrepresented environments.

Where a child requires complex medical, behavioural, mobility, safeguarding, or specialist support, we may require additional assessment, additional staffing, specialist documentation, or approval before accepting the booking.

You may be responsible for loss, damage, injury, cost, or claim arising from your breach of these Terms, inaccurate information, unsafe premises, unauthorised requests, or conduct of household members, guests, pets, or third parties.

We collect and use personal information to manage enquiries, bookings, care plans, safeguarding, payments, client communication, service improvement, and legal compliance.

Information relating to children, health, safeguarding, family circumstances, and care needs may be sensitive. We expect clients to provide such information only where accurate, relevant, and necessary for safe service delivery.

We may keep records of enquiries, booking confirmations, care notes, incident reports, safeguarding notes, complaints, payment records, and communications where needed for operational, legal, insurance, safeguarding, or regulatory purposes.
